BSHOP BH SH Literature Summary

6 COVID

6.1 May 2021

  • Faust JS. Mortality from drug overdoses, homicides, unintentional injuries, motor vehicle crashes, and suicides during the pandemic, march-august 2020. JAMA. 2021.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/Faust. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Authors used national, provisional mortality data to estimate changes due to the pandemic. Suicides were actually lower than expected (95% CI 0.86-0.96), whereas all other causes (excluding MV crashes) were higher. The highest increase in death type was for drug overdoses (95% CI 1.15-1.44).

  • Friedman. Covid-19 and the drug overdose crisis: Uncovering the deadliest months in the united states, january-july 2020. Am J Pub Health. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.2105/AJPH.2021.306256)
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/Friedman. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: A detailed look at drug overdoses during the pandemic. Horrific. The increases were seen across the board but there was a lot of variability in the degree of increase by state.

  • Morton CM. Alcohol sales during covid-19 social restrictions: Initial evidence from alcoholic beverage control states. Subst Abus. 2021;42(2):158-60.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1080/08897077.2020.1856293
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/Morton. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Analysis of actual alcohol sales during COVID. Great to see some alternatives to survey data but the study only included a few states, so no idea how this looks at the national level.

6.2 April 2021

  • J A. Update: Covid-19 pandemic-associated changes in emergency department visits - united states, december 2020-january 2021. morb Mortal Wkly Rep. 2021;70(15):552-6.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_04/Adjemian. 2021..pdf
    • COMMENT: Documents the decrease in ED visits that occurred but the higher proportion of mental health visits, especially for pediatric cases.

  • RS B. Suicide attempt and intentional self-harm during the earlier phase of the covid-19 pandemic in washtenaw county, michigan. 2021.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_04/Bergmans. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: No increase was found in emergency care for suicide-related behavior during the pandemic; there were actually fewer.

  • Currie JM, Schnell MK, Schwandt H, et al. Trends in drug overdose mortality in ohio during the first 7 months of the covid-19 pandemic. JAMA Netw Open. 2021;4(4):e217112.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2021.7112
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_04/Currie. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: In Ohio there was a spike in overdoses that coincided with the pandemic.

  • Mason M, Arukumar P, Feinglass J. The pandemic stay-at-home order and opioid-involved overdose fatalities. JAMA. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jama.2021.6700
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_04/Mason. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Commentary on COVID and overdose.

  • A V. Symptoms of anxiety or depressive disorder and use of mental health care among adults during the covid-19 pandemic - united states, august 2020-february 2021. morb Mortal Wkly Rep. 2021;70(13):490-4.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_04/Vahratian. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Symptoms of anxiety and depression increased from Aug 2020 to Feb 2021, as did the percentage of people reporting an unmet mental health care need. This is not good news.

6.3 March 2021

  • Gratz KL, Scamaldo KM, Vidana AG, et al. Prospective interactive influence of financial strain and emotional nonacceptance on problematic alcohol use during the covid-19 pandemic. Am J Drug Alcohol Abuse. 2021;47(1):107-16.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1080/00952990.2020.1849248
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_03/Gratz. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Discussion on the reasons for increased drinking during COVID (references some others in this newsletter)

  • Molsberry R, Maskaly J, Reingle Gonzalez JM. Disentangling the root causes of covid-19 related increases in alcohol consumption. Am J Drug Alcohol Abuse. 2021;47(1):1-4.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1080/00952990.2021.1881532
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_03/Molsberry. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Further discussion on reasons for drinking during COVID

  • Perlis RH, Ognyanova K, Santillana M, et al. Association of acute symptoms of covid-19 and symptoms of depression in adults. JAMA Netw Open. 2021;4(3):e213223.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2021.3223
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_03/Perlis. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Over half of a sample that had COVID met criteria for moderate or greater depression. Younger male respondents were more likely to report depression. Adds to work on the psychiatric sequelae of COVID.

  • GM R. Operational stress control during a pandemic: An army national guard perspective on lessons learned during the response to covid-19. mil Behav Health. 2021;9(1):11-6.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1080/21635781.2021.1904064
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_03/Reger. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Reger discusses stress experiences specific to COVID and the National Guard and argues that new COSC doctrine for this group may be necessary. This may be particularly true if we continue to see natural disasters, civil unrest, etc in which the NG is used.

  • Sharma RA, Subedi K, Gbadebo BM, et al. Alcohol withdrawal rates in hospitalized patients during the covid-19 pandemic. JAMA Netw Open. 2021;4(3):e210422.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2021.0422
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_03/Sharma. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: This was the first report of alcohol withdrawal related to COVID (using data). Withdrawal was higher during the stay at home period compared to the same time during 2019. The analysis here isn’t super rigorous (e.g., rates were higher during the pre stay-at-home during 2020, too) but these suggest an increase.

  • Shreffler J, Shoff H, Thomas JJ, et al. Brief report: The impact of covid-19 on emergency department overdose diagnoses and county overdose deaths. Am J Addict. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1111/ajad.13148
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_03/Shreffler. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: In KY, overdoses and overdose deaths increased during the pandemic.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1176/appi.ps.202000393)
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Bernardini. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Brief discussion on the intersection of SDOH and COVID-19

  • Families BS. Pain points poll deep dive: Understanding the mental health impact of covid-19 on mental health. 2021.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Blue Star Families. 2021.b..pdf
    • COMMENT: Poll given during COVID. Brief report. Also includes some recommendations.

  • Families BS. Pain points poll deep dive: Understanding the impact of covid-19 on blakc and hispanic/latinx families. 2021.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Blue Star Families. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Subreport on the impacts of COVID on Black and Latino families in particular. Nice to see this focus.

  • Czeisler ME, Lane RI, Wiley JF, et al. Follow-up survey of us adult reports of mental health, substance use, and suicidal ideation during the covid-19 pandemic, september 2020. JAMA Netw Open. 2021;4(2):e2037665.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2020.37665
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Czeisler. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: This study suggests that mental health-related adverse effects of COVID-19 did not disappear with time. It begs the question about the sheer impact this will have and for how long moving forward.

  • Holland KM, Jones C, Vivolo-Kantor AM, et al. Trends in us emergency department visits for mental health, overdose, and violence outcomes before and during the covid-19 pandemic. JAMA Psychiatry. 2021;78(4):372-9.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2020.4402
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Holland. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: ED visits for mental health conditions, suicide attempts, drug overdoses, intimate partner violence, and child abuse and neglect were higher during the COVID-19 pandemic than the same period in 2019

  • McKetta S, Morrison CN, Keyes KM. Trends in us alcohol consumption frequency during the first wave of the sars-cov-2 pandemic. Alcohol Clin Exp Res. 2021;45(4):773-83.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1111/acer.14575
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/McKetta. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Drinking days increased during the first months of the pandemic.

  • LR M-E. Racial and ethnic disparities in the prevalence of stress and worry, mental health conditions, and increased substance use among adults during the covid-19 pandemic - united states, april and may 2020. Morb Mortal Wkly Rep. 2021;70(5):162-6.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/McKnight-Eily. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: There were notable disparities, especially among black and Hispanic/Latino.

  • Patel SY, Mehrotra A, Huskamp HA, et al. Variation in telemedicine use and outpatient care during the covid-19 pandemic in the united states. Health Aff (Millwood). 2021;40(2):349-58.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1377/hlthaff.2020.01786
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Patel. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Exhibit 3 is worth checking out. Social work, psychiatry, and psychology saw the lowest disruptions in total patient visits per week and also were among the most commonly delivered via telemedicine.

  • Wang Y, Kala MP, Jafar TH. Factors associated with psychological distress during the coronavirus disease 2019 (covid-19) pandemic on the predominantly general population: A systematic review and meta-analysis. PLoS One. 2020;15(12):e0244630.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0244630
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Wang. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: FINALLY! A meta-analysis on this topic. At this point (it’s old, December 2020), it included 68 studies. It provides estimates and risk factors for various BH outcomes. Moving forward, I will probably focus on pulling reviews or meta-analyses on this topic.

6.5 January 2021

  • Breslau J, Finucane ML, Locker AR, et al. A longitudinal study of psychological distress in the united states before and during the covid-19 pandemic. Prev Med. 2021;143:106362.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.ypmed.2020.106362
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Breslau. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Another COVID-19 longitudinal survey on distress.

  • Jager J, Keyes KM. Is substance use changing because of the covid-19 pandemic? Conceptual and methodological considerations to delineating the impact of the covid-19 pandemic on substance use and disorder. Addiction. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1111/add.15414
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Jager. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: A very in the weeds commentary about time/period effects in relation to COVID and alcohol/drug use. Pulled because as the authors note, trying to get at COVID’s impacts is very challenging and requires complex modeling.

  • Jetelina KK, Knell G, Molsberry RJ. Changes in intimate partner violence during the early stages of the covid-19 pandemic in the USA. Inj Prev. 2021;27(1):93-7.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1136/injuryprev-2020-043831
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Jetelina. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Cross-sectional survey examining intimate partner violence during COVID in the US. Major limitations in how the survey was distributed (i.e., not representative) but provides some data that may be of interest. Am still curious if FAP has seen any changes during COVID in partner/child abuse numbers.

  • Nagata JM, Ganson KT, Whittle HJ, et al. Food insufficiency and mental health in the U.S. During the covid-19 pandemic. American Journal of Preventive Medicine. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.amepre.2020.12.004
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Nagata. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Food insufficiency increased during COVID and was associated with poorer mental health. The relationship between food insufficiency and mental health was weaker among those who received free groceries/meals.

  • Nemani K, Li C, Olfson M, et al. Association of psychiatric disorders with mortality among patients with covid-19. JAMA Psychiatry. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2020.4442
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Nemani. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Patients with schizophrenia spectrum disorders but not mood/anxiety disorders were more likely to die after testing positive for COVID. Keep in mind this is one study and it didn’t look at all BH diagnoses. There are a few others out there on this topic that are more comprehensive.

  • Calderon-Anyosa RJC, Kaufman JS. Impact of covid-19 lockdown policy on homicide, suicide, and motor vehicle deaths in Peru. Prev Med. 2021;143:106331.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.ypmed.2020.106331
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Renzo. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Study of out Peru showing drops in homicides, suicides, traffic accidents, and other accidental deaths during their COVID lockdowns.

  • Faust JS, Shah SB, Du C, et al. Suicide deaths during the covid-19 stay-at-home advisory in Massachusetts, march to may 2020. JAMA Netw Open. 2021;4(1):e2034273.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2020.34273
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Samuel. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Expected suicides did not differ in Massachusetts during the stay-at-home advisory compared to previous years.

  • Wang QQ, Kaelber DC, Xu R, et al. Covid-19 risk and outcomes in patients with substance use disorders: Analyses from electronic health records in the United States. Mol Psychiatry. 2021;26(1):30-9.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1038/s41380-020-00880-7
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Wang. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Large EHR study showing greater risk of COVID and adverse outcomes for those with SUDs. Similar dataset as has been previously published but focuses on SUDs here.

6.6 December 2020

  • Ammerman BA, Burke TA, Jacobucci R, et al. Preliminary investigation of the association between covid-19 and suicidal thoughts and behaviors in the u.S. Journal of Psychiatric Research. 2021;134:32-8.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.jpsychires.2020.12.037
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Ammerman. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: COVID and suicidal thoughts/behaviors survey. Very small sample drawn from AMZ mechanical Turk. Shows expected links between COVID experiences and STBs. What was new (and odd) was they found some respondents reported intentionally exposing themselves to COVID with intent to die. This is the first I had heard of this. Definitely need replication.

  • Bray et al. Racial differences in statewide suicide mortality trends in Maryland during the coronavirus disease 2019 (covid-19) pandemic. JAMA Psychiatry. 2020.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Bray. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: An early look at the impact of COVID on suicide. This analysis shows that the impacts on suicide depend on race. Black residents in Maryland saw increases in suicide whereas White residents saw decreases. Understanding the impact of this pandemic on suicide and related issues is going to require this kind of stratification, as we know there are huge differences in how and who it’s affecting.

  • Cuellar A, Mark TL, Sharfstein SS, et al. How to mitigate the mental health care consequences of the covid-19 financial crisis. Psychiatr Serv. 2020;71(12):1317-9.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1176/appi.ps.202000329
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Cuellar. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Brief paper describing economic impacts on BH and suicide, with recommendations for risk mitigation. Not really an issue for Soldiers but more applicable for their families.

  • Friedman et al. Overdose-related cardiac arrests observed by emergency medical services during the us covid-19 epidemic. JAMA Psychiatry. 2020.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Friedman. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Brief report describing surge in overdose-related cardiac arrests during the early months of the pandemic. Noteworthy that overall overdose incidents didn’t increase here, suggesting the fatality rate may be higher (e.g., more lethal drug supply, isolation leading to solo use).

  • Huskamp et al.. Treatment of opioid use disorder among commercially insured patients in the context of the covid-19 pandemic. JAMA. 2020.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Huskamp. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: This analysis shows that early in the pandemic, there were slightly fewer individuals initiating treatment with medications for opioid use disorder. Those who were already taking medications for opioid use disorder did not see disruptions. Of note, most were for buprenorphine and it’s unclear if this included opioid treatment programs, which may have experienced disruptions.

  • Leske S, Kõlves K, Crompton D, et al. Real-time suicide mortality data from police reports in queensland, australia, during the covid-19 pandemic: An interrupted time-series analysis. The Lancet Psychiatry. 2021;8(1):58-63.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/s2215-0366(20)30435-1
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Leske. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: One of the first (albeit small) studies coming out on the impacts of COVID on suicides. This one was done in Queensland and found no evidence of a change in suicide due to COVID. They also saw no changes in the motives for suspected suicides. Check out the Bray article this month though for an example of how the overall rate may be masking important subgroup changes.

  • Magill E, Siegel Z, Pike KM. The mental health of frontline health care providers during pandemics: A rapid review of the literature. Psychiatr Serv. 2020;71(12):1260-9.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1176/appi.ps.202000274
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Magill. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Another review of pandemic impacts on BH in health care workers. At this point there must be 10 or more of these published!

  • Murata S, Rezeppa T, Thoma B, et al. The psychiatric sequelae of the covid-19 pandemic in adolescents, adults, and health care workers. Depress Anxiety. 2020.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1002/da.23120
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Murata. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Survey on COVID experiences and psychiatric symptoms. Adolescents reported greater symptoms than adults. Loneliness was the most common predictor across all outcomes.

  • SAMHSA. Executive order report: Saving lives through increased support for mental and behavioral health needs. 2020.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/SAMHSA.2 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: This report highlights needs stemming from the COVID-19 pandemic and provides a blueprint to alleviate ongoing challenges. This is a high-level summary document but the recommendations from various agencies are noteworthy. The DODs input was a little underwhelming (IMO)

6.7 November 2020

  • Alexander GC, Tajanlangit M, Heyward J, et al. Use and content of primary care office-based vs telemedicine care visits during the covid-19 pandemic in the us. JAMA Netw Open. 2020;3(10):e2021476.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2020.21476
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/NOV/Alexander. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: A very detailed analysis of in-person and telemedicine primary care before and during the pandemic. The focus is on characterizing changes as a whole - nationally - as opposed to one specific area (e.g., very little on BH here). Nevertheless, it’s a useful backdrop for understanding everything else.

  • Anestis MD, Bond AE, Daruwala SE, et al. Suicidal ideation among individuals who have purchased firearms during covid-19. American Journal of Preventive Medicine. 2020.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.amepre.2020.http://doi.org/10.013
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/NOV/Annestis. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: An interesting analysis comparing those who purchased a firearm during COVID, firearm owners who did not purchase one during COVID, and non-firearm owners. Those who purchased a firearm during COVID more frequently reported suicidal ideation. Findings on firearm storage practices across groups were mixed but those who purchased during COVID made changes to their practices that increased risk (e.g., making them more readily accessible). Study has major limitations (e.g., not representative, not differentiating first-time purchasers) but highlights a potentially high-risk group resulting from COVID.

  • Goldman ML, Druss BG, Horvitz-Lennon M, et al. Mental health policy in the era of covid-19. Psychiatr Serv. 2020;71(11):1158-62.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1176/appi.ps.202000219
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/NOV/Goldman. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Brief overview of policy changes that have occurred in the area of mental health in response to COVID. Areas include: legislation, regulation, financing, accountability, and workforce development.

  • Gollust SE, Vogel RI, Rothman A, et al. Americans’ perceptions of disparities in covid-19 mortality: Results from a nationally-representative survey. Prev Med. 2020;141:106278.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.ypmed.2020.106278
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/NOV/Gollust. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: We of course know there are a variety of COVID disparities. This study asked a representative US population about their views and awareness of COVID disparities (age, comorbidity, race, and SES). Age and health morbidities were much more recognized than racial and SES disparities. Differences along education, income, party affiliation, and news sources explained some of the knowledge gaps.

  • John A, Pirkis J, Gunnell D, et al. Trends in suicide during the covid-19 pandemic. BMJ. 2020;371:m4352.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1136/bmj.m4352
