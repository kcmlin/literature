BSHOP BH SH Literature Summary

7 Depression

7.1 December 2020

  • Cuijpers P, Pineda BS, Quero S, et al. Psychological interventions to prevent the onset of depressive disorders: A meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials. Clin Psychol Rev. 2020;83:101955.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.cpr.2020.101955
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Cuijpers. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Large review of prevention interventions for depression. Overall, prevention interventions were associated with a 19% decrease in developing a depressive disorder. The average NNT was 21.

7.2 November 2020

  • Davis AK, Barrett FS, May DG, et al. Effects of psilocybin-assisted therapy on major depressive disorder: A randomized clinical trial. JAMA Psychiatry. 2020.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2020.3285
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/NOV/Davis. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: I remember discussing the inclusion criteria for this trial’s protocols way back when during our brown bag lunch meetings! Rigorous work and very promising results. “This RCT found that psilocybin-assisted therapy was efficacious in producing large, rapid, and sustained antidepressant effects in patients with major depressive disorder”.

  • Guidi J, Fava GA. Sequential combination of pharmacotherapy and psychotherapy in major depressive disorder: A systematic review and meta-analysis. JAMA Psychiatry. 2020.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2020.3650
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/NOV/Guidi. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Sequent integration of psychotherapy after acute medication was associated with reduce risk of relapse and recurrent of MDD in this large meta-analysis of 17 RCTs (2283 participants).

7.3 October 2020

  • Rigabert A, Motrico E, Moreno-Peral P, et al. Effectiveness of online psychological and psychoeducational interventions to prevent depression: Systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials. Clin Psychol Rev. 2020;82:101931.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.cpr.2020.101931
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/OCT/Rigabert. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Online prevention interventions were found to have small effects in reducing depressive symptoms. Variability in the outcomes was explained by indicated prevention and interactive interventions (both with higher effectiveness).

7.4 August 2020

  • Milak MS, Rashid R, Dong Z, et al. Assessment of relationship of ketamine dose with magnetic resonance spectroscopy of glx and gaba responses in adults with major depression: A randomized clinical trial. JAMA Netw Open. 2020;3(8):e2013211.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2020.13211
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/Milak. 2020..pdf
    • COMMENT: Ketamine’s anti-depressant effects appear mediated by suppression of glutamate/glutamine

  • Okereke OI, Reynolds CF, 3rd, Mischoulon D, et al. Effect of long-term vitamin d3 supplementation vs placebo on risk of depression or clinically relevant depressive symptoms and on change in mood scores: A randomized clinical trial. JAMA. 2020;324(5):471-80.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jama.2020.10224
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/Okereke.2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Large RCT of vitamin D supplementation among older adults did not find it prevented incidence or recurrence of depression/depressive symptoms over a reasonably long follow-up around 5 years.

  • Theriault FL, Gardner W, Momoli F, et al. Mental health service use in depressed military personnel: A systematic review. Mil Med. 2020;185(7-8):e1255-e62.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1093/milmed/usaa015
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/Theriault. 2020..pdf
    • COMMENT: Although not a surprise, there was a large treatment gap (less than half with depression sought care). Consistent with CIVs. Extent of the treatment gap depended on the measures used (screenings vs. diagnostic interview).
