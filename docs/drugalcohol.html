BSHOP BH SH Literature Summary

8 Drug and Alcohol

8.1 May 2021

  • Adams RS, Garnick DW, Harris AHS, et al. Assessing the postdeployment quality of treatment for substance use disorders among army enlisted soldiers in the military health system. J Subst Abuse Treat. 2020;114:108026.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.jsat.2020.108026
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/Adams. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: A rare look at the substance use treatment outcomes within the Army. Following return from deployment, those in the AC versus NG/R were more likely to initiate and engage in treatment. Having an index diagnosis in a specialty BH setting was the strongest predictor of engaging in treatment. The data are old (2008-2011) but the integration of SUDCC and BH has hopefully driven up initiation and engagement. If anyone’s used the HEDIS measures, please email me as I have questions about them!

  • Bentzley BS, Han SS, Neuner S, et al. Comparison of treatments for cocaine use disorder among adults: A systematic review and meta-analysis. JAMA Netw Open. 2021;4(5):e218049.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2021.8049
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/Bentzley. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: In this large meta-analysis of 157 clinical trials, contingency management was the only approach associated with negative cocaine urinalysis results.

  • Delgado MK, Shofer F, Wetherill R, et al. Accuracy of consumer-marketed smartphone-paired alcohol breath testing devices: A laboratory validation study. Alcohol Clin Exp Res. 2021;45(5):1091-9.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1111/acer.14597
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/Delgado. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Intended for researchers/clinicians but consumer-marketed BAC devices deviate from gold standard BAC measures!

  • DiMaggio. The role of alcohol and others drugs on emergency department traumatic injury mortality in the united states. Drug Alcohol Depend. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.drugalcdep.2021.108763
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/DiMaggio. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Nationally representative data show an increase in alcohol and drug-related ED injury discharges from 2006-2012. The risk of traumatic injury death is increased in alcohol and drug-related injuries. I pulled this because I was unaware that there was actually a debate about the role of alcohol/drugs in the mortality in trauma cases in EDs.

  • Kilian C, Manthey J, Carr S, et al. Stigmatization of people with alcohol use disorders: An updated systematic review of population studies. Alcohol Clin Exp Res. 2021;45(5):899-911.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1111/acer.14598
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/Kilian. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: SUDs include additional stigma above and beyond BH. From the abstract: “Compared to substance-unrelated mental disorders, persons with AUD were generally less likely to be considered mentally ill, while they were perceived as being more dangerous and responsible for their condition. Further, the public desire for social distance was consistently higher for people with AUD.” AUDs and DUDs didn’t differ.

  • Magill E. The principles and practices of psychoeducation with alcohol and drug use disorders: A review and brief guide. J Subst Abuse Treat. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.jsat.2021.108442 10.1016/
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/Magill. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Psychoeducation is a “brief process of therapy focused on the communication of varied aspects of disease- and/or treatment-related information”. In the Army, it’s carried out through the ADAPT program. This paper reviews the literature on psychoeducation and identifies key principles and practices across methods.

  • Matson TE, Carrell DS, Bobb JF, et al. Prevalence of medical cannabis use and associated health conditions documented in electronic health records among primary care patients in washington state. JAMA Netw Open. 2021;4(5):e219375.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2021.9375
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/Matson. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Applicable for Veterans but sharing because all of our Soldiers will be them some day! EHR study showing that patients using medical cannabis have conditions for which it might be indicated but also contraindications that should be discussed.

  • McNeely J, Adam A, Rotrosen J, et al. Comparison of methods for alcohol and drug screening in primary care clinics. JAMA Netw Open. 2021;4(5):e2110721.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2021.10721
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/McNeely. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: A real world multi-site quality improvement study on drug and alcohol screening in primary care. Suggest (intuitively) that screening at every visit will lead to higher screening rates than annual screenings. Re-emphasizes the need for screenings to be self-administered (versus staff-administered), as detection rates were higher among the self-administered sites.

  • Mintz CM, Hartz SM, Fisher SL, et al. A cascade of care for alcohol use disorder: Using 2015-2019 national survey on drug use and health data to identify gaps in past 12-month care. Alcohol Clin Exp Res. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1111/acer.14609
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/Mintz. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Nice study using NSDUH data to characterize the alcohol cascade of care. Originally applied to HIV, this framework has been useful for OUD and now here to highlight the well-known gap in the Screening, Brief Intervention, and Referral to Treatment (and engagement) for alcohol. The gaps in the cascade have been shown previously in the Army but with older data. Would be nice to take a look at more recent data. A major takeaway is that there’s a massive gap between screening and any kind of meaningful intervention (counseling, referral, treatment, etc).

  • O’Donaghue. Trends in filled naloxone prescriptions before and during the covid-19 pandemic. JAMA Health Forum. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamahealthforum.2021.0393
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/O’Donaghue. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Brief report documents decreases in filled nlx prescriptions that were not explained by decreases in opioid prescribing. Although nlx is dispensed outside of pharmacies, this drop is one of many factors that might explain the drug overdose epidemic that only got worse during covid.

  • E. P. The number needed to prescribe - what would it take to expand access to buprenorphine? N Engl J Med. 2021;384(19):1783-4.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/Poorman. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Brief commenary on recent laws to expand buprenorphine prescribing. This about sums it up: “Loosening restrictions will lead to some progress. But much of the effort to expand buprenorphine prescribing and access will depend on physicians believing that people with a substance use disorder aren’t just “addicts,” but are people with a chronic medical disease that we can and should treat — and backing up this belief with our prescriptions."

  • Livne. Religiosity and substance use in us adults: Findings from a large-scale national survey. Drug Alcohol Depend. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.drugalcdep.2021.108796
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/New folder/Livne. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: NESARC study showing that frequency of religious service attendance was protective for SUDs (but importance of religion and religious affiliation were not). There’s a huge literature showing that access to activities that are inconsistent or don’t promote drug/alcohol use (e.g., going to church, mosque, etc!) decrease drug/alcohol use.

  • Midgette G, Kilmer B. Can novel “swift-certain-fair” programs work outside of pioneering jurisdictions? An analysis of 24/7 sobriety in montana, USA. Addiction. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1111/add.15584
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/New folder/Midgette. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Some folks at RAND have been evaluating a swift, certain, fair program (SCF) for DUIs. I’ve followed this a bit more closely because its approach (frequent monitoring, punitive) does seem like something that could be piloted in the military (perhaps for drinking-related incidents as opposed to DUIs).

  • Murphy CE, Wang RC, Montoy JC, et al. Effect of extended-release naltrexone on alcohol consumption: A systematic review and meta-analysis. Addiction. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1111/add.15572
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_05/New folder/Murphy. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Extended-release (injectable) naltrexone decreases heavy drinking days and the number of drinking days, but the effect sizes are modest. Nice to see some numbers put on the effect sizes here. AUD medications are under prescribed in general but this shows clearly that they have a positive effect.

8.2 April 2021

  • Fairbairn CE, Bosch N. A new generation of transdermal alcohol biosensing technology: Practical applications, machine learning analytics, and questions for future research. Addiction. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1111/add.15523
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_04/Fairbairn. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Review of transdermal alcohol monitors and research. Not directly applicable to our work at the moment but an interesting area in general that someday may trickle down into our work.

8.3 March 2021

  • Fairley M, Humphreys K, Joyce VR, et al. Cost-effectiveness of treatments for opioid use disorder. JAMA Psychiatry. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2021.0247
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_03/Fairley. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Detailed cost-effectiveness study for OUD. Expanding medications for OUD, overdose education and naloxone, and contingency management are all cost-effective (and life-saving)

  • Peterson C, Li M, Xu L, et al. Assessment of annual cost of substance use disorder in us hospitals. JAMA Netw Open. 2021;4(3):e210242.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2021.0242
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_03/Peterson. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Study estimates the annual medical costs of SUDs in US hospitals to be 13.2 billion. Costs per encounter were relatively similar across substances but because of higher encounters, total alcohol costs were 3x higher than the next closest substance (opioids).

  • Strain EC. Hegemony, homogeneity, and dsm-5 sud. Drug Alcohol Depend. 2021;221:108660.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.drugalcdep.2021.108660
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_03/Strain. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: A nice commentary on the strengths and limitations of DSM 5 for SUDs.

  • ND V. Prevalence of substance use disorders by time since first substance use among young people in the us. JAMA Psychiatry. 2021.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_03/Volkow. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Brief report demonstrating and emphasizing the relationship between earlier drug use and development of SUDs. Supports screening in adolescence.

8.4 February 2021

  • Beetham T, Saloner B, Gaye M, et al. Admission practices and cost of care for opioid use disorder at residential addiction treatment programs in the us. Health Aff (Millwood). 2021;40(2):317-25.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1377/hlthaff.2020.00378
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Beetham. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: This study identified worrying practices used by residential programs for OUD (e.g., admitting patients without an intake evaluation, requiring high upfront payments).

  • Gray JC, Larson MJ, Moresco N, et al. The association of engagement in substance use treatment with negative separation from the military among soldiers with post-deployment alcohol use disorder. Drug Alcohol Depend. 2021;221:108647.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.drugalcdep.2021.108647
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Gray. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Soldiers with AUD post-deployment initiated (40%) and engaged (24%) in SUD treatment at low levels. Those who engaged in treatment were more likely to have a negative separation compared to those who did not engage in treatment.

  • Hughto JMW, Quinn EK, Dunbar MS, et al. Prevalence and co-occurrence of alcohol, nicotine, and other substance use disorder diagnoses among us transgender and cisgender adults. JAMA Netw Open. 2021;4(2):e2036512.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2020.36512
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Hughto. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Compared to cisgender adults, transgender adults have much higher SUDs (nicotine, alcohol, and drugs)

  • McKay JR. Impact of continuing care on recovery from substance use disorder. Alcohol Res. 2021;41(1):01.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.35946/arcr.v41.1.01
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/McKay. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Review of continuing care in addictions. I don’t have a great sense of how well the Army implements these approaches. I know it’s gotten much better than the past (when one could “graduate” treatment) though.

  • Stanciu CN, Brunette MF, Teja N, et al. Evidence for use of cannabinoids in mood disorders, anxiety disorders, and ptsd: A systematic review. Psychiatr Serv. 2021;72(4):429-36.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1176/appi.ps.202000189
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Stanciu. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: There’s insufficient evidence that cannabinoids (THC or CBD) reduce symptoms for mood, anxiety, or posttraumatic stress disorders.

  • Vest BM, Brady LA, Brimmer MJ, et al. Variations in risk and motivations for substance use over the course of military service. Substance use & misuse. 2021;56(4):559-66.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1080/10826084.2021.1887257
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Vest. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Qualitative study that highlights how the military lifespan interacts with substance use.

  • Vivolo-Kantor AM. Defining indicators for drug overdose emergency department visits and hospitalizations in icd-10-cm coded discharge data. Inj Prev. 2021;27:i56-i61.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Vivolo-Kantor. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Paper gives detailed review of CDC guidance on coding overdose data. Could be useful to have on hand for substance use work. Would also seem to be something that could be tracked on some recurring basis for monitoring/surveillance.

  • Warren EC, Kolodny A. Trends in heroin treatment admissions in the united states by race, sex, and age. JAMA Netw Open. 2021;4(2):e2036640.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2020.36640
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/2021_02/Warren. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Study shows some interesting differences in treatment admission by age and race. There are a lot of ways to interpret these.

8.5 January 2021

  • Compton WM, Valentino RJ, DuPont RL. Polysubstance use in the U.S. Opioid crisis. Mol Psychiatry. 2021;26(1):41-50.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1038/s41380-020-00949-3
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Compton. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Short perspective highlighting why it’s important to consider polysubstance use in addressing the opioid epidemic.

  • Han B CJ, Etz K. Methamphetamine overdose deaths in the US by sex and race and ethnicity. JAMA Psychiatry. 2021;Online first.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Han. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Brief report documenting increase in methamphetamine-related overdoses. This has been on the radar for some time. This study doesn’t address it but there are some gaps in terms of understanding if this is driven by fentanyl tampering in stimulant supply. The last thing the US needs is a stimulant-type epidemic for which there are no medications to use for treatment.

  • Hoopsick RA, Homish DL, Vest BM, et al. Resilience to hazardous drinking among never-deployed male United States army reserve and national guard soldiers. Alcohol Clin Exp Res. 2021.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1111/acer.14561
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Hoopsick. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Super small sample but showed that marital satisfaction and “psychological hardiness” buffered some emotional effects on some measures related to hazardous drinking. Not a lot out there with this population (Reserve/NG) so pulled it, even though it’s a bit complicated.

  • Rogers AH, Zvolensky MJ, Ditre JW, et al. Association of opioid misuse with anxiety and depression: A systematic review of the literature. Clin Psychol Rev. 2021;84:101978.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.cpr.2021.101978
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Rogers. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: A useful reference should we do work at the intersection of OUD and anxiety/depression.

  • Rowe CL, Santos G-M, Kornbluh W, et al. Using icd-10-cm codes to detect illicit substance use: A comparison with retrospective self-report. Drug and Alcohol Dependence. 2021:108537.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.drugalcdep.2021.108537
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/JAN/Rowe. 2021.pdf
    • COMMENT: Comparison of ICD codes with self-reported drug use in ED setting. I don’t think we would ever use them this way but they (ICD codes) are not useful measures of substance use.

8.6 December 2020

  • Arkell TR, Vinckenbosch F, Kevin RC, et al. Effect of cannabidiol and delta9-tetrahydrocannabinol on driving performance: A randomized clinical trial. JAMA. 2020;324(21):2177-86.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jama.2020.21218
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Arkell. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Nice crossover study looking at impacts of CBD and CBD/THC combinations on driving performance. Showed that CBD-dominant use didn’t impair driving performance (with some caveats, particularly with CBD:THC ratios matching real-world scenarios).

  • Ochalek TA, Cumpston KL, Wills BK, et al. Nonfatal opioid overdoses at an urban emergency department during the covid-19 pandemic. JAMA. 2020.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jama.2020.17477
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Cance. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Brief report investigating changes in outpatient buprenorphine dispensing in Texas after federal regulations relaxed prescribing laws because of COVID. There was an increase in new patients but a decrease in existing patients. The data aren’t very detailed (from a PDMP) and it’s hard to make sense of what drove each of these. Regardless, hopefully COVID will lead to new federal laws that put fewer limits on this lifesaving treatment.

  • Kosten TR, Petrakis IL. The hidden epidemic of opioid overdoses during the coronavirus disease 2019 pandemic. JAMA Psychiatry. 2020.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2020.4148
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Kosten. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: An interesting commentary that weaves together the COVID pandemic and the long history of vaccine development for addictions, which have not progressed as one would hope.

  • Mintz CM, Hartz SM, Fisher SL, et al. A cascade of care for alcohol use disorder: Using 2015-2018 national survey on drug use and health data to identify gaps in care. medRxiv. 2020.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1101/2020.10.30.20222695
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Mintz. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: I don’t normally do pre-prints but this one was relevant to an ongoing DOD IG audit on alcohol screening and access to treatment for AUDs. Nationally, there’s decent coverage for screening but the translation of at-risk individuals into brief intervention and treatment is extremely low. This problem exists in the military as well.

  • Morgan JR, Barocas JA, Murphy SM, et al. Comparison of rates of overdose and hospitalization after initiation of medication for opioid use disorder in the inpatient vs outpatient setting. JAMA Netw Open. 2020;3(12):e2029676.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2020.29676
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Morgan. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Observational comparative effectiveness study of inpatient versus outpatient care for OUD. Outpatient care had lower risks of hospitalizations and overdoses. Patients were matched using propensity scores. This study supports increasing capacity for outpatient treatment, particularly with medications, and careful consideration of what subgroups need and would benefit from inpatient treatment.

  • Pearson MR, Wilson AD, Richards DK, et al. Reconsidering alcohol treatment non-responders: Psychosocial functioning among heavy drinkers 3 years following treatment. Addiction. 2020.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1111/add.15280
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Perason. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: The last author on this has been leading a charge for several years now highlighting the heterogeneity in outcomes after alcohol use disorder treatment and challenging the long-standing tradition of using alcohol consumption alone as a treatment success measure. This study shows that high functioning non-responders (i.e., those who do not maintain low drinking levels or complete abstinence) maintained their high functioning status for an additional 2 years. The messaging from a public health perspective versus an individual patient perspective gets messy (but such is life sometimes!).

  • SAMHSA. Preliminary data from the drug abuse warning network - april 2019-october 2020. 2020.

    • LINK (doi):
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/SAMHSA. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: This report describes findings from a national sentinel hospital-based surveillance system. This system was offline for a while but re-instated recently. Pulled this because in the event there is future tampering of drug supply that impact Soldiers (likely), this system should be among the first to detect it and other details (trend, regions, etc).

  • Santo T, Jr., Campbell G, Gisev N, et al. Prevalence of childhood maltreatment among people with opioid use disorder: A systematic review and meta-analysis. Drug Alcohol Depend. 2020;219:108459.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1016/j.drugalcdep.2020.108459
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Santo. 2020.pdf
    • COMMENT: Super comprehensive systematic review and meta-analysis of child maltreatment among persons with OUD. Prevalence rates of abuse are astronomical: women, sexual abuse = 41%; men, sexual abuse = 16%; men and women, physical (43%), emotional (38%), physical (neglect) = 42%, and emotional neglect = 42%. Trauma plays such a huge role in OUD. I think providers know this but the general public and in the military commanders, my guess is less do.

  • Scherrer JF, Tucker J, Salas J, et al. Comparison of opioids prescribed for patients at risk for opioid misuse before and after publication of the centers for disease control and prevention’s opioid prescribing guidelines. JAMA Netw Open. 2020;3(12):e2027481.

    • LINK (doi): http://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2020.27481
    • FILE PATH: P:/CPHE/DEDS/BSHOP/Shared/BH and SH literature/Resources/DEC/Scherrer. 2020.pdf
